HUM•DAH

The Home Mortgage Disclosure Act

Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)

Modernizing government data into useful tools for both experts and the curious.
consumerfinance.gov/hmda ·

Demonstrating its commitment to being a transparent 21st century government agency, Technology + Innovation expanded public access to mortgage lending data with an API-powered application that makes it easy to explore or download data.

HMDA is an API-powered, in-browser data query and analysis tool. It improves the workflow of those in the research and advocacy community, and may even save them a little money from purchasing statistical expensive software. For everyday citizens, it presents a relevant, low-effort data visualization experience. For devs, it demonstrates the capabilities of building a tool using our API.

This was quite the multidisciplinary project. The discovery phase began with a series of needfinding exercises with stakeholders and subject matter experts with the intention of finding shared audience needs and prioritizing them. The aha moment was when we realized we could maximize our work by thinking about whether people wanted to use data to tell stories or listen to one.

NEEDFINDING

HMDA data consists of at least 23 different types of information captured in 130 million records. That's a lot of data. Luckily, at the time that design was onboarded, an API was busy modernizing it away from flat files. Despite having a background in stats, it was a challenge to convey HMDA to the other designers on the team but it prevented us from spinning our wheels. There was a lot of whiteboarding, and a lot of stick figures.

Figuring out the UI was the easy part. The hard part was conveying the information using plain language and presenting it in places where users would need it most. Regular usability sessions helped out with this. Docs. 2013 dataset. Iteration. Happy. Process?
