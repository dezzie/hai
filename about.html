Dezzie wants to make the web a better place.

My work experience has always been curiously about solving people's problems with technology. At the CFPB I have been able to continue advocating for people through planning and moderating UX research, prototyping designs, writing, and helping shape best-practices for a 21st-century government design team. Since I can research, design, and code, I've been known for helping out wherever I can to help something ship on time. To some this makes me a "UX generalist," but I'm actually T-shaped across a few things (I told you my arms were long). In any case, I'd rather just be called a designer. Or just just use my first name. That's what I went by when people didn't call refer to me as a designer but I still solved problems. The only difference now is that I'm not skipping class and I'm getting paid instead.

I wouldn't be a designer if it weren't for having been a die-hard trance music fan since middle school (ask me about that story sometime). But I actively rejected a creative career for ten years after making my first website. I eventually came around to it when I learned that I could use my powers for good. This was when I learned about the CFPB, in a "Surprise!" interview with Audrey Chen (this is also a good story to ask me about).

When not working (but also when working), I like eating, thinking in the abstract, and taking stuff apart. I prefer my music without lyrics.

One day I want to write a history of the Internet, or travel to space.

The typeface on my resume is Arquitecta. The typeface on here is Sanchez. Both were created by Latinotype. Their name is a pun on what they do for a living. It is also a pun for what I am doing right now.
